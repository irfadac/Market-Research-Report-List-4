<p><p>Breast cancer screening involves various types of tests to detect the presence of tumors or abnormalities in the breast tissue. Mammography screening uses low-dose X-rays to capture images of the breast tissue. Breast MRI screening utilizes magnetic fields and radio waves to create detailed images of the breast. Breast ultrasound screening uses sound waves to produce images of the breast tissue. Each screening method has its own advantages and limitations, and they are often used in combination for more accurate results in detecting breast cancer.</p></p>
