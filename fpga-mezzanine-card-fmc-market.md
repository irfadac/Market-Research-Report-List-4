<p><p>The FPGA Mezzanine Card (FMC) market features two primary types: Low Pin Count (LPC) and High Pin Count (HPC) cards. LPC FMCs offer a streamlined interface with fewer pins, making them suitable for cost-sensitive applications requiring moderate performance and connectivity. Conversely, HPC FMCs provide a higher pin count, enabling enhanced data transfer rates and complex functionalities, ideal for demanding applications in telecommunications, signal processing, and high-speed data acquisition. Both types facilitate modular design and scalability in FPGA-based systems.</p></p>
