<p><p>Screws for bone fixation are commonly used in orthopedic surgeries to secure fractured bones. The market for these screws can be categorized into three main types based on the material used: stainless steel, titanium, and bioabsorbable. Stainless steel screws are durable and resistant to corrosion, but may cause interference with imaging tests. Titanium screws are lightweight, biocompatible, and widely used in orthopedic surgeries. Bioabsorbable screws are gradually absorbed by the body, eliminating the need for removal surgeries and reducing the risk of complications.</p></p>
