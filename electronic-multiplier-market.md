<p><p>Electronic multipliers are devices used in scientific instruments to amplify and detect low-level signals. Channel Electron Multipliers (CEMs) are one type of multiplier that utilize multiple channels to amplify incoming particles or photons. Continuous-dynode multipliers are another type, which use a series of dynodes to amplify the signal. Both types of multipliers are commonly used in applications such as mass spectrometry, particle physics research, and astronomy to increase sensitivity and improve signal detection capabilities.</p></p>
