<p><p>Polymer fluorescent microspheres are divided into two main market types: organic and inorganic. Organic fluorescent microspheres are made from natural or synthetic polymers that emit light when excited by an external energy source. In contrast, inorganic fluorescent microspheres are made from materials such as semiconductor nanocrystals that emit light through quantum mechanical processes. Both types of microspheres are widely used in various applications, including biotechnology, medicine, and environmental monitoring, due to their unique fluorescent properties and versatility.</p></p>
