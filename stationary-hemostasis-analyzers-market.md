<p><p>Stationary hemostasis analyzers are medical devices used to measure the ability of blood to clot. There are two main types of detection methods used in these analyzers: optical detection and mechanical detection. </p><p>Optical detection methods utilize light to measure clot formation, while mechanical detection methods rely on sensing changes in viscosity or mechanical resistance during clotting. Both types of analyzers are essential in diagnosing and monitoring bleeding disorders, thrombosis, and other conditions related to blood clotting abnormalities.</p></p>
