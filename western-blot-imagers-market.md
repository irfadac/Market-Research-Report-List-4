<p><p>Western blot imagers come in various types including CCD cameras, which use light-sensitive sensors to detect chemiluminescent signals from Western blots. Laser-based imagers use lasers to excite fluorescently-labeled proteins for detection. X-ray film imagers use X-ray film to capture the image of labeled proteins. Other types of imagers may include CMOS (complementary metal-oxide-semiconductor) cameras or chemiluminescent imagers. Each type of imager has its own advantages and disadvantages, catering to different research needs and preferences in Western blot analysis.</p></p>
