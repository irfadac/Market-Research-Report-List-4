<p><p>Pneumatic clutches are commonly categorized into two main types: Friction Type and Toothed Type. Friction Type clutches use frictional force to transmit power between the driving and driven members, while Toothed Type clutches utilize teeth on the driving and driven members to transfer torque. Friction Type clutches are popular for smooth and continuous power transmission, while Toothed Type clutches are preferred for applications requiring high precision and accurate control of power transmission. Both types offer efficient and reliable performance in various industrial applications.</p></p>
