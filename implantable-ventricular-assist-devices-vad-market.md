<p><p>Implantable Ventricular Assist Devices (VADs) are devices implanted in the heart to help pump blood. There are three main types of VADs: Left Ventricular Assist Devices (LVADs) which assist the left side of the heart, Right Ventricular Assist Devices (RVADs) which assist the right side of the heart, and BiVADs which assist both sides of the heart. Each type of VAD is used to provide circulatory support for patients with heart failure or other heart conditions, improving heart function and overall quality of life.</p></p>

<p><p>Implantable Ventricular Assist Devices (VADs) are used in various applications within the market. Bridge-to-transplant (BTT) refers to using VADs as a temporary solution while a patient awaits a heart transplant. Destination Therapy (DT) involves using VADs as a long-term treatment for patients who are not eligible for a heart transplant. Other applications of VADs include bridge-to-recovery, bridge-to-decision, and bridge-to-candidacy, where they provide support until the patient's condition improves, a decision is made, or they become eligible for a transplant.</p></p>
