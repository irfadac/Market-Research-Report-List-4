<p><p>A concrete test hammer, also known as a Schmidt hammer, is a device used to evaluate the strength of in-place hardened concrete. It operates on the principle that the rebound of an elastic mass is inversely proportional to the hardness of the surface against which the mass strikes. Concrete test hammers are widely used in the construction industry to assess the quality and consistency of concrete structures.</p><p>The global concrete test hammer market is expected to experience steady growth in the coming years, with a projected CAGR of 4.30% during the forecast period.
