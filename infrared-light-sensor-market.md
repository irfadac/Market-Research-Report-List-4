<p><p>The infrared light sensor market is divided into two main types: contact and non-contact light sensors. Contact light sensors require direct physical interaction with the object to detect infrared radiation, making them ideal for applications where proximity is essential. In contrast, non-contact light sensors can detect infrared light from a distance, allowing for remote monitoring and control in various settings, such as security systems and environmental monitoring. Both types play crucial roles across industries, enhancing automation and user experiences.</p></p>
