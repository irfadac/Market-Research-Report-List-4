<p><p>Household non-invasive ventilators are commonly used for treating sleep apnea and respiratory disorders. CPAP (Continuous Positive Airway Pressure) machines provide a constant airflow to keep the airways open during sleep. BiPAP (Bilevel Positive Airway Pressure) devices deliver varying levels of air pressure for inhalation and exhalation. APAP (Automatic Positive Airway Pressure) machines adjust airflow automatically based on the individual's breathing patterns. Each type of ventilator caters to specific needs and preferences of patients, offering effective and comfortable treatment options for respiratory conditions.</p></p>
